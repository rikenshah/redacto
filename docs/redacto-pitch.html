01 · The problem

The 45-second compliance gap nobody has closed

Frontline professionals — nurses, social workers, first responders, journalists — live in a world where the compliant tools require VPN, a separate login, and a network connection. In the 45 seconds between patient visits, they do what every human does. They text.

"TigerConnect may not be appropriate for use in emergency situations." — TigerConnect's own documentation

The gap isn't between secure and insecure channels. The gap is between the moment sensitive content is created and the moment it leaves the device. Nobody has put intelligence in that gap.

Every competitor solves the wrong problem.

They encrypt the channel. We sanitize the content. That is not a feature difference — it is a category difference.

05 · Why this can't be easily copied

Four structural advantages.

01
Zero-trust by architecture
On-device inference is not a UX choice — it's the only architecture where PHI is physically incapable of leaving the device. No cloud competitor can claim this. By definition.
02
Channel-agnostic output
Redacted text works on Messages, WhatsApp, Slack, Email — any channel. You don't onboard recipients. You sanitize, then send anywhere. TigerConnect requires both ends on the platform.
03
Relational LLM reasoning
"Lisa" is not PHI. "The patient's daughter Lisa" is. This relational disambiguation requires semantic reasoning — exactly what quantized Gemma on the Snapdragon NPU delivers at sub-500ms.
04
No BAA required
No third-party processor = no Business Associate Agreement needed. Every cloud redaction tool requires weeks of legal review. ShieldText removes an entire compliance layer — structurally, not by policy.
10-second pitch to any judge
"Every other solution protects the pipe. We protect what goes through it — before it goes anywhere. That makes every channel safe, works offline, and requires no agreement from the recipient. No competitor can claim all three."
06 · Technical stack

Built on the Snapdragon 8 Elite.

Layer 1 · Speed
Regex safety net
High-speed deterministic catch for SSNs, phone numbers, email patterns. Runs in parallel with the LLM as a redundant safety layer.
Layer 2 · Intelligence
LiteRT-LM + Gemma
Quantized Gemma (4-bit INT) running on-device via LiteRT-LM. Catches relational identifiers, contextual PHI, and role-based redaction that regex cannot touch.
Layer 3 · Silicon
Snapdragon NPU
Hexagon NPU via Qualcomm QNN delegates. 80+ TOPS delivers sub-500ms inference for the "Deep Reasoning" layer — entirely offline, no radio active.
Vision · Input
Google ML Kit OCR
On-device text recognition from photos of medical charts, prescriptions, IDs, handwritten notes. Feeds directly into the redaction pipeline.
UI · Interface
Jetpack Compose
Android native UI with share sheet extension, confidence heatmap overlay, and mode selector. Works as a standalone app and OS-level share target.
Device · Target
Samsung S25 Ultra
Snapdragon 8 Elite (SM8750), 12GB RAM, Android 15. GPU path via OpenCL for demo. NPU path via libLiteRtDispatch_Qualcomm.so once compiled from source.
